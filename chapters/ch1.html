Basics of Islam

Foundation of the Muslim faith

What is Islam?

Islam is the same truth that God revealed through all His prophets to every people. For one fifth of the world's population, Islam is both a religion and a complete way of life. Muslims follow a religion of peace, mercy, and forgiveness.

What does Islam mean?

The Arabic word 'Islam' simply means submission and derives from a word meaning peace. In a religious context it means peace acquired by submitting your will to Almighty God — Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ. 'Allah' is the Arabic name for God, used by Arab Muslims and Christians alike.

What is the Quran?

The Quran is a record of the exact words revealed by Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ through the Angel Jibreel عليه السلام to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ fourteen centuries ago. It was memorised, dictated to the Companions, and written down by scribes who cross-checked it during his lifetime. Not one word of its 114 chapters has been changed over the centuries.

What are Hadeeth?

Hadeeth are oral traditions relating to the words, deeds, and tacit approvals of the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. They are the second source of Islam after the Quran, helping us understand its application. Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ says in the Quran that in the life of the Prophet ﷺ there is the best of examples.

The Islamic System of Life

1. Tawhid (Oneness of Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ)
The most important Islamic belief. Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ is All-knowing, All-wise, All-powerful — the First and the Last, with no partners or son. He gives life and takes it away. We must return to Him after death.
2. Risalah (Prophethood)
The channel of communication between Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ and mankind. Allah sent His guidance through chosen prophets from the beginning of Creation until the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ, the final Messenger.
3. Akhirah (Life after Death)
Our life on earth is temporary — a test for the never-ending Hereafter. Life is meaningless without the Day of Judgment (Yawmal Akhirah) in the court of Almighty Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ.

The 6 Articles of Faith (Arkan ul-Iman)

Eman — the first Pillar — contains six articles that every Muslim must believe in. These form the complete belief system (Aqeedah) of Islam.

لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا ٱللَّٰهُ مُحَمَّدٌ رَسُولُ ٱللَّٰهِ
Lā ilāha illAllāhu Muḥammadur Rasūlullāh
"There is no God but Allah, and Muhammad ﷺ is His last Messenger."
I
☀️
Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ

Believe that Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ is One, with no partners or equals. He is unique, unlike any of His creation — perfect and eternal in every way. He is the First and the Last, the All-Knowing, the All-Seeing.

One GodNo partnersEternal
II
✨
Angels (Malāikah)

Created from light (Noor), they obey Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ perfectly and never disobey. Key angels include:

  • Jibreel عليه السلام — brought wahy (revelation) to the Prophets
  • Mikail عليه السلام — in charge of rain and food
  • Israfil عليه السلام — will blow the trumpet on Judgement Day
  • Izrail عليه السلام — the angel of death
  • Malik عليه السلام — guardian of Hell
  • Ridwan عليه السلام — guardian of Heaven
  • Kiraman Katibeen عليهما السلام — record every person's deeds
  • Munkar & Nakir عليهما السلام — question the deceased in the grave
Made of NoorNever disobey
III
📖
Revealed Books (Kutub)

Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ revealed scriptures to guide humanity. The Quran (final and perfectly preserved) supersedes all earlier books:

  • Zaboor (Psalms) — given to Prophet Dawood عليه السلام
  • Suhuf (Scrolls) — given to Prophet Ibrahim عليه السلام
  • Taurat (Torah) — given to Prophet Musa عليه السلام
  • Injeel (Gospel) — given to Prophet Eesa عليه السلام
  • Al-Quran — given to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ

All previous books were altered over time. Only the Quran remains perfectly preserved.

Quran is finalUnaltered

🕌
Prophets (Anbiyā')

Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ sent thousands of prophets — from Adam عليه السلام (first) to Muhammad ﷺ (last and final). We believe in all of them. Among those named in the Quran: Ibrahim عليه السلام, Nuh عليه السلام, Musa عليه السلام, Dawood عليه السلام, Sulayman عليه السلام, and Eesa عليه السلام.

Adam عليه السلام — firstMuhammad ﷺ — last

Day of Resurrection (Qiyāmah)

All people — believers and disbelievers — will be resurrected in real flesh and blood on the Day of Judgement. They will stand before Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ to be judged on every deed done in this world.

Everyone raisedFull accountabilityReal bodies

Predestination (Qadar)

Everything in the universe runs on a course already known to Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ. He has complete knowledge of all things — past, present, and future. This does not remove human free will; we are still responsible for our choices.
