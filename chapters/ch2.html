Namaz, Taharah & Ghusl

Purification and prayer in Islam

I. Ghusl (Full Body Purification)

Ghusl is the full ritual bath that removes major ritual impurity (hadath al-akbar). It is obligatory in certain situations and should be performed carefully.

Fara'id of Ghusl — the 3 obligatory acts

If any of these three acts is omitted, the Ghusl is invalid and must be repeated.
1
Rinse the mouth — gargle water thoroughly to clean the entire inner mouth
2
Irrigate the nose — rinse inside the nose with water and blow out
3
Wash the entire body — pour water over the whole body ensuring every part is wet

Sunnah of Ghusl — the complete method of the Prophet ﷺ

  • Wash both hands up to the elbows
  • Wash the private parts (using the left hand) and remove any filth from the body
  • Perform complete Wudhu (ablution)
  • Pour water over the head three times, rubbing the hair so water reaches the roots
  • Wash the body starting with the right side, then left — rubbing so water reaches everywhere

When is Ghusl Obligatory?

Ghusl becomes obligatory (fardh) after: intimate marital relations or sexual discharge · completing the menstrual cycle or postpartum bleeding · accepting Islam (for new converts) · in preparation for Hajj or Umrah.

II. Tayammum (Dry Purification)

When water is genuinely unavailable or its use would cause harm, Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ has provided an alternative — striking clean earth and wiping the face and hands.

Seven circumstances when Tayammum is allowed

  • Access to water is restricted or impractical
  • Sufficient water for washing is not available
  • Using the water would leave insufficient drinking water (risking illness or thirst)
  • Obtaining water is hazardous or prohibitively expensive
  • Using water poses a health or medical risk
  • The available water is impure (naajis)
  • Time for prayer would expire before obtaining water

Procedure of Tayammum

1
Find clean ground — rock, sand, or any surface that naturally collects dust, free of najaasah
2
Make the intention (niyyah) — intend to perform tayammum for purification
3
Say Bismillah — begin in the name of Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ
4
Strike both hands on the surface of the ground once
5
Dust off the hands and rub them together
6
Wipe the face with both hands, then wipe the back of each hand

III. Wudhu (Ablution)

Wudhu is required before every Salah. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Allah does not accept the prayer of one who has broken his wudhu until he performs it again." (Bukhari)

1
Intention & Bismillah — make intention to purify for salah and say Bismillah
2
Hands ×3 — wash both hands up to wrists, starting with right; clean between fingers
3
Mouth ×3 — rinse the mouth three times, spitting the water out
4
Nose ×3 — sniff water into the nose using the right hand, blow out with left
5
Face ×3 — wash from hairline to chin, ear to ear; run fingers through beard if present
6
Arms ×3 — right arm up to and including elbow, then left. No part should remain dry
7
Head (Masah) ×1 — pass wet hands over the hair from front to back (do not wipe over a cap)
8
Ears ×1 — index fingers inside ears, thumbs wipe the back
9
Feet ×3 — right foot first up to and including the ankles; clean between the toes

Dua after completing Wudhu:

أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ
Ash-hadu allā ilāha illAllāhu waḥdahu lā sharīka lah, wa-ash-hadu anna Muḥammadan 'abduhu wa rasūluh
I testify there is none worthy of worship except Allah alone with no partner, and that Muhammad ﷺ is His slave and Messenger.

IV. Azaan (Call to Prayer)

The Azaan is the Islamic call to prayer broadcast five times daily. Bilal ibn Rabah رضي الله عنه was the first and beloved Muadhdhin (caller to prayer) of the Prophet ﷺ.

Dua after Azaan

اللَّهُمَّ رَبَّ هَذِهِ الدَّعْوَةِ التَّامَّةِ وَالصَّلَاةِ الْقَائِمَةِ آتِ مُحَمَّدًا الْوَسِيلَةَ وَالْفَضِيلَةَ وَابْعَثْهُ مَقَامًا مَحْمُودًا الَّذِي وَعَدْتَهُ
Allāhumma rabba hādhihid-da'watit-tāmmah, waṣ-ṣalātil-qā'imah, āti Muḥammadanil-wasīlata wal-faḍīlah, wab'ath-hu maqāman maḥmūdanilladhī wa'adtah
O Allah, Lord of this perfect call and the prayer to be established — grant Muhammad ﷺ the means and the honour, and raise him to the praised station You have promised him.

V. Namaz (Salah)

Prayer is the Muslim's direct communication with Allah سُبْحَانَهُ وَتَعَالَىٰ. The Prophet ﷺ said: "The first thing a servant will be called to account for on the Day of Judgement is Salah." (Tirmidhi)

Prayer Times

Fajr

Dawn → Sunrise

True dawn until the sun appears

Dhuhr

Zenith → Shadow = height

After sun passes its midday peak

Asr

Shadow = height → Sunset

Afternoon until just before sunset

Maghrib

Sunset → Twilight

Immediately after sun sets

Isha

Twilight → Midnight

When red glow fully disappears

Arkan of Salah (Pillars — must be performed)

Takbir al-Ihram
Opening "Allahu Akbar" — begins the prayer
Qiyam
Standing upright while reciting (if able)
Qira'ah
Recitation of Surah Al-Fatiha in every rak'ah
Ruku'
Bowing — back parallel to ground, hands on knees
I'tidal
Standing straight after bowing
Sujood
Prostration — forehead, nose, palms, knees & toes on ground
Final Tashahhud
Reciting Tahiyat in the final sitting
Tasleem
"As-salāmu 'alaykum wa raḥmatullāh" — ends the prayer

Steps of Salah — Hanafi School

1
Prepare — Clean clothes, clean pure place, clear mind of worldly thoughts.
2
Face the Qibla — Stand facing the Ka'aba. Feet evenly spaced, eyes looking at the spot where the head will touch in Sajdah.
3
Niyyah (Intention) — Know which prayer and how many rakats. Done in the heart — no need to say aloud.
4
Takbiratul Ihram — Raise hands to ear lobes (men) or shoulders (women), say "Allahu Akbar" aloud. Prayer has now begun.
5
Qiyam — Fold hands: right over left, below the navel (men) or at chest level (women). Recite opening du'a, then Surah Al-Fatiha, then another Surah.
6
Ruku' — Say "Allahu Akbar," bow with hands on knees, back parallel to ground. Recite Subhāna Rabbiyal-'Aẓīm three times.
7
I'tidal — Rise from bow saying "Sami'allāhu liman ḥamidah" then "Rabbanā lakal-ḥamd." Stand fully upright.
8
First Sujood — Say "Allahu Akbar," prostrate: forehead, nose, both palms, knees and toes all touching the ground. Recite Subhāna Rabbiyal-A'lā three times.
9
Sitting — Rise to sitting position saying "Allahu Akbar." Pause briefly. Then perform second Sujood identically.
10
Second rak'ah — Rise for the second rak'ah (same as first, without Takbiratul Ihram). After second Sujood, remain seated.
11
Tashahhud — Recite the Tahiyat in the seated position. If only 2 rak'ahs, follow with Durood Ibrahim and Du'a.
12
Tasleem — Turn right and say "As-salāmu 'alaykum wa raḥmatullāh", then left and repeat. The prayer is complete.

Tashahhud (Tahiyat)

التَّحِيَّاتُ لِلَّهِ وَالصَّلَوَاتُ وَالطَّيِّبَاتُ ۚ السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكَ أَيُّهَا النَّبِيُّ وَرَحْمَةُ اللَّهِ وَبَرَكَاتُهُ ۚ السَّلَامُ عَلَيْنَا وَعَلَى عِبَادِ اللَّهِ الصَّالِحِينَ ۚ أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ
At-taḥiyyātu lillāhi waṣ-ṣalawātu waṭ-ṭayyibāt. As-salāmu 'alayka ayyuhan-Nabiyyu wa raḥmatullāhi wa barakātuh. As-salāmu 'alaynā wa 'alā 'ibādillāhiṣ-ṣāliḥīn. Ash-hadu allā ilāha illAllāhu wa ash-hadu anna Muḥammadan 'abduhu wa rasūluh.
All worships — oral, physical and monetary — are for Allah. Peace be upon you, O Prophet ﷺ, and Allah's mercy and blessings. Peace be on us and on all righteous servants of Allah. I bear witness there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness Muhammad ﷺ is His servant and Messenger.

Things to Do on Jumu'ah (Friday)

Personal Hygiene
  • Perform Ghusl
  • Wear good clean clothes
  • Apply perfume (men)
  • Use miswak
  • Trim nails
Recite Surah Kahf

"Whoever reads Surah al-Kahf on Friday will be illuminated with light between the two Fridays." (Narrated by Haakim and al-Bayhaqi — classed Saheeh)

Be Productive
  • Read Qur'an
  • Pray Nafilah
  • Make Dhikr
  • Make Du'a abundantly
Jumu'ah Etiquette
  • Attend the Masjid early
  • Sit as close as possible to the Imam
  • Listen attentively to the Khutbah
  • Send abundant Salawat upon the Prophet ﷺ
Sa'at al-Istijabah

"Friday is comprised of twelve hours. There is one in which a Muslim who asks Allah for something will have it granted. Seek it in the last hour after Asr." (Narrated by Jabir — Saheeh)
