Topic 1 — Social Media & Youth

Case Study 1: Privacy vs. Exposure
Scenario: Azzam is a social media fanatic — he uses all apps and posts 4–5 stories daily about his lifestyle. His friend Jabbar raises concerns about the amount of data collected by platforms, stating that companies and even governments use this data for tracking and advertising. Azzam responds: "If I have nothing to hide, then why should I be concerned about privacy?"
Discussion Questions
  1. Do you find an issue with Azzam's lifestyle?
  2. What would you do if you encountered a person like Azzam in your organisation or friend circle?
  3. How do you refute Azzam's statement: "If I have nothing to hide, why should I be concerned about privacy?"
Case Study 2: Dependency on Social Media
Scenario: Rija, a student, has been using Instagram for two years and averages over 3 hours of daily screen time. As exams approach, her parents ask her to give up her phone for 20 days. Rija refuses, saying she needs WhatsApp for study material and that it's impossible to function today without social media.
Discussion Questions
  1. Roti, Kapda, Makan… Education and Healthcare were added as basic needs. Should social media be added to this list?
  2. Is it possible to have a social life or build meaningful relationships without social media today?
  3. Can you imagine your life without social media? If yes, how is it different? If not, why?
Case Study 3: Information vs. Mental Health
Scenario: Ravish, a graduate student, stays updated on current affairs through social media and calls it the "One Click Information Hub." His friend Dinesh disagrees, saying social media is damaging reading habits and mental health, contributing to anxiety, ADHD, and depression.
Discussion Questions
  1. Are you with Ravish or Dinesh? Explain your reasoning.
  2. In this era of technology, is ignorance a choice?
  3. Has social media affected your mental health or that of someone you know?
  4. Can a person be well-informed without using social media?
Case Study 4: Image vs. Reality
Scenario: Zunik is a billionaire with over 2 million followers. His Instagram is filled with images of abs, yachts, and luxury. He posts over 30 stories a day. When his friend John calls him a narcissist and claims his profile is fake, Zunik is annoyed.
Discussion Questions
  1. Is self-image and self-portraiture on social media a new form of Takabbur (arrogance)?
  2. Do you ever feel jealousy or inadequacy when seeing others' social media profiles?
  3. Do you agree with the phrase, "Jo dikhta hai, wohi bikta hai" (Only what is seen, sells)?
  4. What influences your decision to post something on social media?

Topic 2 — Dawah: Interfaith & Intra-faith Dialogue

Case Study 1: Dealing with an Atheist
Scenario: After a lecture on worship, you find yourself in a discussion with an atheist friend who questions the very idea of God and doesn't find the concept meaningful. You wish to explain the Islamic worldview without sounding harsh or disrespectful.
Common Questions an Atheist May Ask
  1. What evidence is there for the existence of God?
  2. How do you know the Quran is the word of Allah and not written by humans?
  3. If everything is predestined by Allah, how can humans be accountable for their actions?
Discussion Points
  • Use logical reasoning and examples from nature, the universe, and human consciousness
  • Emphasise the Quran's literary and scientific uniqueness
  • Clarify the concept of Qadar (destiny) alongside human free will and responsibility
Case Study 2: Dealing with a Hindu Friend
Scenario: You and your Hindu classmate become friends. During a lunch break near a masjid, you go to pray while he waits. Upon your return, he asks about the obligation of praying five times a day and why charity (Zakah) is made compulsory in Islam.
Discussion Points
  • Explain prayer as discipline, remembrance of God, and spiritual purification
  • Describe Zakah as a system to uplift society, ensure social justice, and purify wealth
  • Maintain respect and avoid comparing religions directly
Case Study 3: Dealing with a Christian Friend
Scenario: Over a coffee break, you explain that Islam is not a new religion from the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ but the continuation of the same message from Adam عليه السلام, with finality in the Quran. Your Christian friend becomes more curious.
Common Questions
  1. Why don't Muslims follow the Bible completely if it was revealed to Jesus عليه السلام?
  2. What is the core belief of Islam?
Discussion Points
  • Belief in all previous prophets and scriptures — but previous scriptures were altered over time
  • Introduce Tawheed (Oneness of God), Prophethood, and the Afterlife as the core pillars
  • Show common ground while emphasising the finality of the Quran
Case Study 4: Dealing with a Muslim Peer
Scenario: On a secular campus, you meet a group of Muslim students who are not practising. They invite you to their circle, but you politely decline. Another classmate later confronts you, saying you missed a chance to influence them positively.
Discussion Points
  • The importance of protecting one's Iman while maintaining respectful ties
  • Reflect on the balance between self-preservation and gentle dawah
  • Dawah is a process — must be done with wisdom and sincerity
